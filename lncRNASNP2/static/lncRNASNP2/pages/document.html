What is lncRNASNP?

LncRNASNP is a database to provide a comprehensive resource of variants on human lincRNAs. It contains variants including SNPs, TCGA cancer mutations and CosmicNCVs in lncRNAs, structure change by SNP, function change by mutations, the binding sites of lncRNA:miRNA and potential functional variants which can impact lncRNA:miRNA interaction. In addition, lncRNASNP also integrates GWAS data and miRNA expression data into functional SNP selection for genetic association studies. Currently, users can:

  • Browse or search SNPs in lncRNAs and their impacts on lncRNA;
  • Browse or search Cosmic noncoding variants and their effects on lnRNA;
  • Browse or search TCGA mutations in lncRNAs and their effects on lncRNAs;
  • Browse or search miRNA binding sites on lncRNAs;
  • Browse or search miRNA binding sites on lncRNAs;
  • Browse or search SNPs in GWAS linkage disequilibrium (LD) regions;
  • Browse or search miRNA expression data to prioritize functional SNP selection;
  • Browse lncRNA expression in diseases;

Pipeline to construct lncRNASNP
The definition of conservation target sites in our database

The regions of lncRNA exons were submitted to UCSC liftover tools, setting the minimum ratio of bases that must remap >50%, then convert to mouse, rat, dog assemblies. If one exon has output regions in other genome, we consider it conserved in other genome. Then, the conserved exon regions were submitted to UCSC Tables and we got MultiZ multiple alignments of the four species. Targetscan was used to predict the miRNA targets on these sequences and the miRNA binding sites existed in human, mouse, rat and dog alignment sequences are classified as conserved.

The definition of GWAS tagSNP linkage disequilibrium (LD) region in our database
GWAS tagSNPs were downloaded from GWAS catalog website,then we used the SNAP (SNP Annotation and Proxy Search) to get the proxy SNP of each tagSNP,LD regions of each tagSNP is caculated from proxy snps,the population panels include ASW,CEU,CHD,GIH,LWK,MEX,MKK,TSI and YRI.
Info of CosmicNCV (Cosmic Nocoding Variants)
CosmicNCV data were downloaded from Cosmic (Catalogue Of Somatic Mutations In Cancer), The mutation impact introduced in COSMIC v73 have been derived from the new FATHMM-MKL algorithm. This algorithm predicts the functional, molecular and phenotypic consequences of protein missense variants using hidden Markov models.
About TCGA Mutation data
TCGA mutation data in our database was downloaded from TCGA using firehose_get (https://confluence.broadinstitute.org/display/GDAC/Download), cancer types include ACC, BRCA, CESC, CHOL, COADREAD, DLBC, ESCA, GBM, GBMLGG, HNSC, KICH, KIRC, KIRP, LAML, LGG, LIHC, LUAD, LUSC, VO, PAAD, PCPG, PRAD, READ, SARC, SKCM, STAD, TGCT, THCA, THYM, UCEC, UCS, UVM. The mutation effects on lnRNA functions were analyzed by FATHMM.
Description of RNAsnp parameters
In lncRNASNP2, we used RNAsnp to predict SNPs effects on lncRNAs, The program RNAsnp helps for an effective detection of local RNA secondary structural changes induced by the SNPs. RNAsnp starts with the computation of base pair probabilities for wild-type and mutant sequence (with SNP) by using the RNAfold or RNAplfold tools from Vienna RNA package.Further, the stuctural difference between the wild-type and mutant is assessed by computing the Pearson correlation coefficient and the Euclidean distance for all sequence intervals. The sequence interval with maximum distance or minimum correlation is finally reported with its p-value. This empirical p-value is computed using the background distribution given the sequence length, G+C content and SNP position.
